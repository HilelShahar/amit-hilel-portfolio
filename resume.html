GlassesUSA — Research for first physical retail store

  • Led qualitative research for an online eyewear company's first store (Boston): in-depth interviews with insurance-holding customers, a full journey map (pre-visit → eye exam → frame selection → checkout → post-purchase), and thematic coding in NVivo.
  • Found the eye exam (medical) and frame purchase (emotional) felt disconnected; manual pen-and-paper insurance bred mistrust; strong-prescription customers couldn't evaluate frames without lenses.
  • Recommendations redesigned the exam-to-purchase transition, digitized the insurance flow, and added visualization tools — reducing friction at key conversion points.

Arduino Studio — Discovery for seed-stage no-code hardware startup

  • First researcher; advocated for discovery when the team wanted to skip to development. Ran user interviews, competitive analysis, and cognitive walkthroughs, learning embedded systems from scratch.
  • Found "no-code" tools still required code at critical points and carried high cognitive load. Designed a behavior-based interaction model (visual trigger-condition-action flows), templates covering 80% of use cases, and plain-language guidance.

Tel Aviv Municipality — "My DigiTel" resident portal

  • Mixed-methods usability research across payments, services, transportation, parking, and classes (mobile + desktop). Moderated interviews and unmoderated Lookback sessions, segmented by age and device.
  • Residents expected personalization but found a generic interface; external redirects caused disorientation and abandonment. Recommended global search, fewer redirects, location/interest personalization, and accessibility improvements.

Tel Aviv Municipality — 106+ WhatsApp reporting tool

  • Evaluated a WhatsApp-based municipal service via a hybrid approach: moderated Zoom sessions plus 10+ days of unmoderated longitudinal real-world use, capturing both authentic behavior and reflective insight before rollout.

Healthcare organization — Flu vaccination behavioral research

  • Found low vaccination was friction, not resistance: info was buried and booking required active searching. Proposed auto-scheduling with opt-out — few canceled, most showed up, significantly increasing rates while preserving choice.
